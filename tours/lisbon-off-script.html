N°01 / Six ways into Portugal Lisbon · Mouraria · Alfama

The city locals
keep for themselves.

An opening

Lisbon is two cities. The one written about, and the one that begins around midnight, two streets back from any hotel. We can show you the second.

Four moments — not a schedule

What you will remember.

A Tuesday, late
Fado, in a room of nine.

Mouraria. No sign on the door. A singer who has performed for three presidents and would prefer not to discuss it.

Before sunrise
Praça do Comércio, empty.

The square as Lisboetas know it — before the trams, before the cruise lines, before anyone has decided what kind of day it will be.

Friday, six in the evening
A studio above Chiado.

A designer most of Paris has heard of, and most of Lisbon hasn't yet. A glass of vinho verde. You leave with a coat.

After midnight
The neighbourhood changes face.

A rooftop no app lists. A second one, smaller, that the first one's owner sometimes mentions. A way home through streets that feel borrowed.

The kind of evening you couldn't have arranged yourself, and won't be able to describe afterwards.
— A guest, Spring 2025
